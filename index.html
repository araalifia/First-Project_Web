History

Scuderia Ferrari was founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1929, initially as a racing team for Alfa Romeo. It became an independent manufacturer in 1947. The team made its Formula 1 debut in the 1950 Monaco Grand Prix. Throughout its rich history, Ferrari has been synonymous with speed, innovation, and fierce competition on the track.

The team achieved its first World Championship win with Alberto Ascari in 1952. Since then, Ferrari has secured numerous titles and has been home to some of the most iconic drivers in the sport, including Niki Lauda, Michael Schumacher, and Kimi Räikkönen.

Achievements

Ferrari holds several records in Formula 1, including the most Constructors' Championships with 16 titles. They have also won 15 Drivers' Championships. Notable periods of dominance include the 1970s with Niki Lauda and the early 2000s with Michael Schumacher, who won five consecutive drivers' titles from 2000 to 2004.

The team's legacy is also marked by its iconic cars, such as the 500 F2, 312T, and F2004, each representing a milestone in the evolution of Formula 1 technology and performance.

Drivers

Scuderia Ferrari has been home to some of the most talented drivers in Formula 1 history. Currently, the team features Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz, who represent the next generation of Ferrari's pursuit of championship glory.

Charles Leclerc

Charles Leclerc is a young driver from Monaco who joined Ferrari in 2019. Known for his speed and strategic racing, Leclerc has quickly established himself as one of the top drivers on the grid. As a product of the Ferrari Driver Academy, he has shown exceptional performance and is considered one of the future stars of Formula 1.

Carlos Sainz

Carlos Sainz is a Spanish driver who joined Ferrari in 2021. Before his time with Ferrari, Sainz drove for teams like McLaren, Renault, and Toro Rosso. He is known for his consistency and ability to adapt to new cars, making him a valuable asset for Ferrari's ongoing quest for success.

Technical Information

Ferrari's success on the track is not just due to its drivers but also its relentless pursuit of technical excellence. The team is known for its innovative engineering, with a dedicated team of engineers and designers working on every aspect of the car, from aerodynamics to power units.

The current car, the SF24, is powered by a hybrid V6 engine, combining high performance with improved fuel efficiency. Ferrari continues to push the boundaries of technology in Formula 1, developing new solutions that not only enhance performance but also contribute to the future of automotive technology.
